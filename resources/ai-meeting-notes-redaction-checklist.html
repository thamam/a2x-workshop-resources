Redaction rule

Keep the useful pattern; remove the real-world identifiers.

AI can help turn notes into decisions and next actions, but workshop tools should not receive raw transcripts, attendee names, customer names, employee records, production incidents, account screenshots, private links, contracts, credentials, pricing details, regulated records, confidential strategy, or proprietary source material. Work from a redacted packet that preserves the workflow pattern and decision context.

Five-pass redaction

Clean the notes before prompting

1 · Remove identifiers

Names, accounts, and exact records

Replace people, customers, vendors, tickets, IDs, URLs, screenshots, and account labels with generic roles or fictional labels.

2 · Remove secrets

Anything that grants access

Delete credentials, tokens, private repository links, billing pages, support portals, internal dashboards, and configuration snippets.

3 · Blur sensitive facts

Numbers, incidents, contracts, and strategy

Convert exact metrics, contract terms, incident details, regulated records, and confidential plans into broad categories or safe ranges.

4 · Preserve decisions

Keep what AI needs to reason

Retain the meeting purpose, decision options, generic constraints, owner roles, review gates, open questions, and intended next step.

5 · Add a review gate

Make output provisional

Name who must review the AI summary, which source notes they should compare against, and what should stay as “needs clarification.”

Safe replacements:
Use role labels, generic workflow names, approximate ranges, fictional examples, public docs, approved samples, or “details withheld pending owner review.”
Prompt boundary:
Ask AI to work only from the redacted packet, avoid inventing commitments, flag uncertainty, and label every action item with an owner role plus review need.
