Baseline rule

A useful baseline is a comparison point, not a business case.

Record the current workflow pattern, friction, review path, and observable signals using only generic or approved examples. Do not include customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, credentials, regulated data, vendor contracts, pricing, private repository links, raw prompts, incident transcripts, or confidential strategy.

Baseline lenses

Five safe things to measure before the pilot

Workflow shape

What happens today?

  • What repeated task, handoff, or review does the workflow include?
  • How often does it happen in generic terms: daily, weekly, monthly, or ad hoc?
  • Where does work wait, bounce, or get rechecked?
Human effort

Where is attention spent?

  • Which roles draft, review, approve, route, or fix the work?
  • What steps require expert judgment instead of simple formatting?
  • What support questions repeat often enough to notice?
Quality signal

What counts as better?

  • What makes an output usable, incomplete, risky, or wrong?
  • Which reviewer can judge a small sample safely?
  • What decision evidence should appear in the evidence log?
Boundary health

What must stay stable?

  • Which data, systems, roles, and actions are out of scope?
  • What stop condition protects the workflow if quality drops?
  • What rollback or manual fallback exists today?
Adoption friction

What might slow usage?

  • Which users need training, reminders, or office-hours support?
  • What tool, access, policy, or ownership gap could block the first cycle?
  • What question should be escalated before broader use?
How to use it

Run a fifteen-minute baseline pass

Name the workflow slice

Pick one repeated workflow slice from the intake brief or prioritization matrix. Keep it small enough to compare before and after one pilot cycle.

Capture today’s state safely

Use generic ranges, categories, roles, and redacted examples. If useful measurement requires sensitive records, make boundary approval the next step instead of collecting the data here.

Choose review evidence

Select a few observable signals: reviewer decision, cycle status, rework category, support theme, incident count, or completion state. Avoid revenue promises or unverified productivity claims.

Set the comparison date

Decide when to compare the baseline against pilot evidence, value scorecard, health review, or steering decision log.
