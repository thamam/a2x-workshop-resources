Exception rule

A good exception request narrows the risk before it asks for permission.

Do not paste secrets, customer records, employee data, contracts, regulated data, production logs, account screenshots, private repository contents, credentials, or internal strategy into the request. Describe the workflow pattern, the blocked boundary, the sanitized evidence, the proposed guardrails, and the exact decision needed.

Step 1

Fill the seven fields

1. Workflow and owner

Name the repeated workflow, accountable owner, reviewer, and team affected using generic role names. Keep people, customers, tickets, and accounts anonymized.

2. Current boundary

State the rule that blocks action: data type, tool approval, vendor access, retention, procurement, legal review, security control, production system, or public communication boundary.

3. Business reason

Explain the operational pain or decision need without revenue promises, pricing claims, customer names, private metrics, or confidential strategy.

4. Sanitized evidence

Bring safe counts, patterns, toy examples, redacted snippets, or workshop exercises that show why the request matters. If proof requires sensitive material, say what safer review environment is needed.

5. Requested exception

Ask for the smallest permission that would answer the question: one tool, one workflow, one safe sample class, one reviewer group, one time box, and one output type.

6. Guardrails and rollback

Define allowed inputs, off-limits inputs, human review, retention/cleanup, logging expectations, incident route, rollback owner, and pause trigger.

7. Decision deadline

Name who can approve, clarify, reject, or redirect the request and when the decision will be revisited.

Step 2

Choose the request state

Ready to route

The request is narrow, sanitized, owner-backed, time-boxed, and has a rollback plan. Send it to the named policy, security, legal, procurement, or sponsor reviewer.

Clarify first

The workflow value is plausible, but the boundary, owner, evidence, or guardrail is vague. Shrink the scope before asking for approval.

Stop or redirect

The request requires sensitive material in an unapproved tool, production action without rollback, public claims, customer messaging, or unclear authority. Move to an approved environment or a non-AI workaround.
