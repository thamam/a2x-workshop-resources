A2X field kit · meeting follow-up

Turn meeting notes into safe next actions.

Use this handout after a workshop, pilot review, vendor demo, steering meeting, or adoption check-in. It helps teams use AI to organize notes without letting private details, weak evidence, or vague ownership become an action list.

Action-capture rule

Summarize patterns first, then assign only reviewed, reversible next actions.

Do not paste raw transcripts, customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, private repository contents, credentials, regulated records, vendor contracts, pricing claims, or internal strategy into a shared AI tool. Use sanitized bullets, fictional examples, redacted excerpts, or already-approved notes. If the action requires sensitive context, record the owner and approval path instead of the sensitive detail.

Step 1

Prepare the safe note packet

Keep

Decision facts

Include the meeting purpose, non-sensitive decisions, generic workflow names, dates, owners by role, and approved next review point.

Redact

Useful patterns

Convert real examples into categories: blocker type, support theme, evidence gap, action owner, or decision state.

Remove

Sensitive specifics

Remove names, customer details, raw records, screenshots, credentials, contract terms, private URLs, production identifiers, and unapproved claims.

Step 2

Prompt AI for a draft action list

Reusable prompt: “You are helping convert sanitized meeting notes into a reviewed action list. Use only the notes below. Return: decisions made, open questions, action items with owner role and due signal, risks or approval needs, and suggested next review. If evidence is missing, say ‘needs clarification’ instead of guessing. Do not invent commitments, dates, metrics, pricing, or approvals.”

Step 3

Review before sending or tracking

Check source support

Every action should map to an actual meeting note, decision, or owner statement. Delete actions that appear only because the AI “helpfully” inferred them.

Check ownership

Replace person names with accountable roles when sharing outside the immediate team, then confirm the real owner in the approved internal system.

Check reversibility

Prefer the smallest next step: draft, review, clarify, score, schedule, or prepare. Escalate before any production, contract, customer, or policy-impacting action.

Check evidence gaps

Mark weak items as “needs clarification” rather than turning uncertainty into a task. Use the output review rubric if the draft will be circulated.
