Pre-mortem rule

Invent the failure pattern, not the confidential example.

Do not paste customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, contracts, pricing, private repository links, raw prompts, confidential strategy, credentials, or regulated records into this exercise. Describe generic workflow patterns, safe sample categories, likely human mistakes, review gaps, and response owners only.

Failure lanes

Rehearse the obvious ways the workflow can drift

1 · Input

Unsafe or unclear inputs arrive

The user pastes sensitive details, vague context, stale files, or examples the reviewer cannot verify.

2 · Output

The answer sounds right but is wrong

The draft is plausible, incomplete, overconfident, outdated, or missing evidence that a human reviewer needs.

3 · Review

The human gate gets skipped

People treat an AI draft as a decision, forward it too soon, or cannot tell who owns the final check.

4 · Scope

The pilot expands quietly

A toy or shadow-mode test starts touching new data, users, tools, or decisions before approval catches up.

5 · Operations

Support and rollback are missing

Users do not know who to ask, how to pause, what fallback process to use, or what evidence proves recovery.

6 · Memory

Lessons vanish after the demo

Evidence, assumptions, decisions, and cleanup duties are not captured, so the same risk returns later.

Keep the rehearsal lightweight

Start with three failures

  • Pick one input risk.
  • Pick one review or scope risk.
  • Pick one operations or memory risk.

Name the first tripwire

  • Choose the earliest visible warning.
  • Define who sees it.
  • Write the pause or escalation route.
