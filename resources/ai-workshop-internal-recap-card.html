Recap rule

Share direction and boundaries, not private examples.

Do not include customer records, employee data, production logs, screenshots, account pages, private repository links, contracts, vendor pricing, raw prompts, raw AI outputs, confidential strategy, credentials, regulated records, or personal attendee notes. Summarize only the public-safe workflow pattern, why it matters, what was learned, what is explicitly off-limits, and the next reversible action.

Recap lanes

Choose the message your team actually needs

1 · Inform

People need a clean summary

Explain the workshop theme, the safest useful workflow pattern, and the boundaries that keep the next discussion productive.

2 · Align

People need one owner

Name the owner role, reviewer role, and next forum so the idea does not become an orphaned AI experiment.

3 · Request

A tiny next step needs support

Ask for a safe sample, review time, access route, or policy answer without asking for broad adoption approval.

4 · Boundary

The important message is restraint

State what will not be pasted, automated, purchased, connected, published, or used in production yet.

5 · Decision

A sponsor needs options

Frame continue, clarify, govern, pause, or close as choices with evidence gaps instead of a recommendation by vibes.

6 · Pause

The safest recap is stop for now

Explain the blocker, the missing approval, and the safer alternative resource or learning path.

Facilitator cues

Use the recap to keep momentum honest

If the team wants to share wins

  • Describe the capability in workflow terms.
  • Attach the review and safety boundary.
  • Avoid outcome guarantees or broad marketing language.

If the team wants approval

  • Ask for the smallest reversible action.
  • Name the evidence that will decide the next move.
  • Route data, access, or procurement questions to the right owner.

If the team is unsure

  • Send the boundary recap anyway.
  • Pick clarify or pause instead of forcing a trial.
  • Use first-week check-in only after a real tiny action exists.
