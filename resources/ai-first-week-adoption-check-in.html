Check-in rule

Use evidence from the first week, not optimism from the workshop.

Do not collect customer records, employee data, raw prompts, raw outputs, transcripts, production logs, account screenshots, pricing, contracts, private repository links, credentials, regulated records, or confidential strategy. Capture patterns, safe evidence categories, owner support needs, reviewer confidence, blockers, and one reversible next action.

Decision signals

Pick the lane that matches the evidence

Continue

Safe value appeared

The team has reviewed output, a visible benefit, a clear owner, and no new boundary concerns. Continue with a slightly clearer next evidence check.

Narrow

The idea is useful but too broad

Reduce scope to one workflow step, one safe sample type, one reviewer, and one stop condition before more people try it.

Support

People are stuck

Route the issue to office hours, a handoff packet, setup help, owner clarification, or prompt-safety review instead of calling the workflow failed.

Escalate

A boundary question appeared

Use policy exception, data boundary, procurement, security, legal, or sponsor review before running more examples.

Pause

The next step needs unsafe data

Stop expansion until the team has an approved tool, approved inputs, accountable reviewer, and cleanup route.

Close

No owner or evidence exists

Record what was learned, keep reusable safe artifacts, and close the experiment without implying the tool or team failed.

Facilitator cues

Make the check-in psychologically safe and operationally sharp

If adoption looks slow

  • Ask whether setup, owner clarity, or reviewer time blocked action.
  • Do not shame non-use; find the missing support route.
  • Choose support or narrow before declaring failure.

If adoption looks fast

  • Check whether boundaries kept pace with usage.
  • Confirm reviewers are seeing outputs before reuse spreads.
  • Keep the next action reversible.

If leadership asks for proof

  • Report evidence categories, not sensitive examples.
  • Separate value signal from rollout recommendation.
  • Use a proof snapshot or decision memo for the next artifact.
