A2X field kit · reviewer calibration

Calibrate reviewers before AI quality becomes a debate.

Use this short session when several people will review AI-generated drafts, summaries, recommendations, code changes, or workflow artifacts. The goal is not to grade the model in the abstract; it is to align humans on what “good enough for the next safe step” means.

Calibration rule

Use sanitized examples, score independently first, then align on evidence and thresholds.

Do not paste customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, private repositories, credentials, regulated records, vendor contracts, pricing claims, or internal strategy into calibration packets. Use toy, fictional, redacted, or already-approved examples. If the team cannot safely share an example, calibrate on the pattern instead.

Step 1

Prepare three sanitized examples

Clear pass

A strong output

Pick an AI output that answers the task, cites or reflects the given source material, names a bounded next step, and stays inside the approved data and action boundary.

Borderline

A useful but risky output

Pick an output that is mostly helpful but has one weakness: vague owner, missing source, overconfident claim, weak rollback path, or unclear review threshold.

Stop

A polished failure

Pick an output that sounds convincing but invents facts, exceeds approval, asks for sensitive data, creates an irreversible action, or makes a claim the team cannot support.

Step 2

Run the 35-minute calibration

Frame the decision

Agree on the workflow, audience, risk level, and the only allowed decisions: approve for next safe step, revise before use, or stop and escalate.

Score independently

Each reviewer scores the same example against task fit, source support, safety boundary, operational clarity, tone/promise control, and reversibility before discussion.

Compare disagreements

Discuss only the review points where scores diverge. Ask: what evidence would make this pass, what boundary makes it stop, and what revision would make it usable?

Write one shared threshold

Convert the discussion into a short rule the team can reuse, such as “no external customer message without source trace and owner approval” or “draft-only until rollback owner is named.”

Repeat once

Run the same process on a second example. If disagreement persists, narrow the workflow or route the review rule to the accountable owner before scaling use.

Step 3

Capture the reusable calibration note

Workflow being calibrated: [generic workflow name].

Examples used: [toy / fictional / redacted / approved sample only].

Review decision labels: [approve next safe step / revise before use / stop and escalate].

Shared pass threshold: [evidence, owner, review gate, rollback path, or source requirement].

Known disagreement: [where reviewers still differ and who decides].

Next evidence to log: [pilot run count, reviewer decisions, support questions, quality misses, or policy escalations].

Do not include: secrets, customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, private repository contents, credentials, regulated records, vendor contracts, pricing claims, or internal strategy.

When to use this

Good triggers for calibration

Before rollout

Use it before more people rely on AI output, especially when one reviewer has been quietly making judgment calls alone.

After a quality miss

Use it when a polished output slipped through review, but keep the example sanitized and focus on the decision rule that failed.

Before a vendor or internal demo becomes a pilot

Use it to decide what evidence the team needs before a demo output is treated as real workflow proof.
