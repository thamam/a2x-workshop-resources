Data-sharing rule

Classify the information category, not the real record.

Keep the conversation at the category level until the boundary is clear. Use toy examples, fictional samples, public docs, or redacted notes first. Do not paste customer records, employee data, production logs, credentials, account screenshots, contracts, private repository links, raw incident details, regulated records, confidential strategy, pricing, or proprietary source material into a workshop tool or demo.

Four-lane sort

Choose the safest next lane

Share

Public or already approved

Use public docs, toy repos, fictional examples, published policies, or explicitly approved training samples. Still keep only the minimum context needed for the task.

Sanitize

Useful pattern, sensitive details

Replace names, IDs, screenshots, private links, exact figures, and live records with generic categories before asking the AI to help.

Ask first

Boundary is unclear

Pause and identify the owner, policy, reviewer, tool setting, retention question, or vendor answer needed before the information enters the tool.

Stop

Not appropriate for this tool

Do not use the data when it includes secrets, regulated records, private customer or employee details, production evidence, contracts, or unapproved source material.

Thirty-second check

Ask these before prompting

1 · Purpose

What job are we asking AI to do?

  • Draft, summarize, classify, debug, compare, or decide?
  • What is the smallest context that supports that job?
  • Can a toy or fictional sample prove the pattern?
2 · Category

What kind of information is involved?

  • Public, internal, confidential, regulated, customer, employee, credential, source, or production evidence?
  • Which category is actually needed?
3 · Tool boundary

Where will it go and who can see it?

  • Which AI tool, account, workspace, vendor, or local environment?
  • Are retention, training, support access, and logs understood?
4 · Review gate

Who can approve the next step?

  • Owner role, reviewer role, policy route, or security/procurement/legal escalation.
  • If nobody owns the answer, stop and use a safer sample.
