Data-use rule

Ask for policies, controls, and evidence — not private examples.

Frame questions around categories: what data may be submitted, whether prompts or outputs are used for training, how long logs are retained, who can access admin/audit views, how deletion works, and what evidence proves the answer. Keep this worksheet generic: no customer records, employee data, production logs, credentials, account screenshots, private repository links, contract terms, pricing, regulated records, incident transcripts, private architecture, or confidential strategy.

Question lanes

Cover the data-use surface area

Inputs
What data categories may users submit?

Ask which categories are allowed, blocked, redacted, or require special approval for the specific workflow.

Training
Are prompts, files, or outputs used to train models?

Ask about default settings, opt-out path, enterprise controls, subprocessors, and how the answer is evidenced.

Retention
How long are prompts, files, outputs, logs, and metadata kept?

Ask about retention windows, admin controls, deletion requests, backup copies, and audit trail availability.

Access
Who can view user content, logs, or account activity?

Ask about vendor support access, internal admins, role permissions, break-glass processes, and reviewer visibility.

Export and deletion
Can the team export, delete, or narrow data later?

Ask how data is returned, removed, de-identified, or scoped down if the pilot pauses, fails, or changes vendor.

Evidence gaps
Which answers are documented versus promised?

Separate verified policy/docs, vendor claims, unknowns, and approval gaps before moving to access or procurement.

Safe question pattern

Turn vague trust questions into answerable checks

1 · Name the workflow

Keep the scope narrow

  • Which repeated workflow is under review?
  • Which user roles need access?
  • Which data categories are proposed — not raw data?
2 · Ask the control question

Focus on settings and behavior

  • What is the default?
  • Can admins change it?
  • What evidence confirms the current setting?
3 · Record confidence

Separate documented from assumed

  • Documented: policy, admin screen, or contract-approved source.
  • Claimed: vendor or owner statement needing proof.
  • Unknown: requires escalation before sensitive use.
4 · Decide the boundary

Do not expand from a guess

  • Allowed now: safe categories only.
  • Needs approval: unclear retention, training, or support access.
  • Stop: no deletion/export path for sensitive workflow data.
