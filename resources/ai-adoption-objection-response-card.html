Objection rule

Do not “handle” the person. Handle the evidence gap.

Do not record names, customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, contracts, vendor pricing, private repository links, raw prompts, confidential strategy, credentials, or regulated records while discussing an objection. Capture the objection category, the evidence needed, the boundary that would make progress safe, the owner, and the smallest reversible next step.

Objection lanes

Classify the concern before answering it

1 · Value

“Is this worth the time?”

Ask what evidence would prove the workflow saves time, improves quality, reduces rework, or clarifies a decision. Route to baseline, value scorecard, or proof snapshot.

2 · Work design

“Will this make the job worse?”

Ask what human role, review gate, support path, or workflow boundary would make the change clearer. Route to change brief, FAQ, or office-hours plan.

3 · Trust

“How do we know it is right?”

Ask what source support, reviewer threshold, calibration step, or audit trail is missing. Route to output review, calibration, acceptance criteria, or audit trail.

4 · Safety

“What if sensitive data leaks?”

Name the data boundary, do-not-paste list, approved tool status, retention rule, and pause trigger. Route to data boundary, prompt safety, policy exception, or cleanup.

5 · Ownership

“Who is accountable?”

Ask which owner, reviewer, sponsor, support path, and steering forum must exist before expansion. Route to owner card, sponsor alignment, steering cadence, or decision log.

6 · Rollback

“What if it goes wrong?”

Ask for the stop signal, rollback path, restart condition, and recovery owner before routine use. Route to rollback drill, incident pause, near-miss debrief, or sunset archive.

Response patterns

Use a calm, evidence-first reply

When the objection is right

  • Name the boundary clearly.
  • Pause or narrow the workflow.
  • Assign evidence before any expansion.

When the objection is vague

  • Ask which lane it belongs to.
  • Turn fear into a testable question.
  • Pick one safe sample or review signal.

When the objection is political

  • Do not argue the person into support.
  • Route to owner, sponsor, or steering decision.
  • Keep the next step reversible.
