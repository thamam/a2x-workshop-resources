Assumption-log rule

If an assumption would change the pilot decision, write it down before the next run.

Do not let “we think,” “the tool should,” “users will,” or “security is probably fine” become hidden operating policy. Keep assumptions public-safe, role-based, and reviewable. Do not record customer details, employee records, private screenshots, credentials, raw prompts, regulated data, contracts, pricing, production logs, or confidential strategy.

Step 1

Sort assumptions by what they can break

Workflow fit

The task behaves as expected

The repeated work has stable inputs, a clear output, and a human reviewer who can spot weak or unsafe results.

Dependency

Another condition must hold

A policy, owner, reviewer, tool access path, sample dataset, support route, or training step must exist before the pilot can continue.

Decision risk

A wrong assumption changes the call

If the assumption fails, the next decision may switch from continue to narrow, retrain, escalate, pause, or stop.

Step 2

Write the five fields that make the assumption testable

Assumption

State what the team believes in plain language: users can review outputs, safe samples are enough, setup friction is manageable, or support can absorb questions.

Dependency

Name what the assumption relies on: role, policy boundary, sanitized input, reviewer capacity, training artifact, tool setting, or meeting cadence.

Evidence check

Choose the smallest signal that can confirm or challenge it: reviewer pass/revise notes, support themes, sample-run result, missing-field count, or escalation count.

Owner and revisit date

Assign a role, not a private name, to check the evidence before the next decision meeting, health review, or rollout-readiness conversation.

Decision trigger

Define what happens if the assumption fails: narrow scope, retrain, update guardrails, request approval, pause the workflow, or stop the pilot.

Step 3

Use AI to find missing assumptions safely

Decision prompt: “Using only the public-safe pilot notes below, identify hidden assumptions that could change the next pilot decision. Return each assumption with dependency, evidence check, owner role, revisit trigger, and fail response. Do not invent private examples, user names, customer details, pricing, approvals, production commitments, or sensitive data.”
