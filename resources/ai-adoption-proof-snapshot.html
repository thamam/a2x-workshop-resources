Proof-snapshot rule

Report patterns and decisions, not private artifacts.

Do not paste customer records, employee data, contracts, regulated data, production logs, account screenshots, private repository contents, credentials, vendor pricing, incident transcripts, raw prompts, or internal strategy. Summarize adoption with sanitized counts, generic workflow categories, reviewer decisions, support themes, and boundary status.

Step 1

Collect the proof categories

Use signal

Who is using it?

Count included users or teams, active users, repeat use, and abandoned use in broad categories rather than names or private work examples.

Quality signal

Did review pass?

Summarize reviewer outcomes: accepted, corrected, rejected, escalated, or unclear. Include only sanitized reasons.

Support signal

Where do users get stuck?

Group office-hours questions, prompt confusion, handoff gaps, data-boundary uncertainty, and ownership friction by theme.

Guardrail signal

Did boundaries drift?

Name incidents, near misses, unclear inputs, missing reviewers, support overload, or scope creep as categories, not raw examples.

Step 2

Write a one-page adoption proof snapshot

Workflow and boundary

Snapshot for: [generic AI workflow]. Included users: [role/team category]. Allowed inputs: [sanitized input categories]. Human review gate: [reviewer/forum].

Observed adoption

Use pattern: [new/repeat/declining use]. Volume: [rough count or range]. Most common moment: [workflow stage]. Abandoned or parked use: [theme].

Quality and review evidence

Reviewer signal: [accepted/corrected/rejected/escalated]. Reusable win: [sanitized pattern]. Fragile failure: [sanitized pattern]. Evidence source: [log/office hours/health review/decision record].

Support and guardrail health

Top support theme: [theme]. Boundary state: [stable/unclear/drifting]. Incidents or near misses: [none/category]. Owner action: [update/narrow/train/pause/escalate].

Decision ask

Recommended next state: continue, update, narrow, retrain, pause, stop, or escalate. Decision owner: [role/forum]. Next evidence needed: [specific sanitized signal by date].

Step 3

Choose the next state

Continue

Adoption is useful, review gates are working, support load is manageable, and boundaries remain stable.

Update or retrain

Users are engaged but repeated confusion, poor handoffs, or review corrections point to a missing aid, prompt rule, or enablement refresh.

Narrow or escalate

Value exists, but the workflow needs a smaller user group, tighter data boundary, stronger owner, or policy/sponsor decision before it grows.

Pause or stop

Boundary drift, weak review, incidents, support overload, or unclear ownership make continued routine use unsafe or not worth the attention.
