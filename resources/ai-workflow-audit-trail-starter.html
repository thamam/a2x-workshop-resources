Audit trail rule

Record categories, decisions, and evidence pointers — not sensitive payloads.

An AI audit trail is useful only if it is safe to keep and easy to inspect. Capture the workflow name, approved input category, tool or model class, human reviewer, decision lane, evidence pointer, and revisit trigger. Do not paste raw prompts, raw model outputs, transcripts, production logs, customer data, employee records, credentials, account screenshots, private repository links, vendor contracts, pricing, regulated records, incident details, or confidential strategy into a shared audit trail unless the approved system and policy explicitly allow it.

Evidence lanes

Decide what belongs in the trail

Context
What workflow and boundary was active?

Name the workflow, safe input category, do-not-use list, tool class, permission rung, and current owner.

Action
What did AI help produce or decide?

Record the output type or decision support category, not the raw content unless the storage location is approved.

Review
Who reviewed it and what was the outcome?

Preserve reviewer role, review rubric used, approve/revise/escalate/stop lane, and one short reason.

Evidence pointer
Where can approved evidence be found?

Point to an approved ticket, document, demo note, or evidence log entry; avoid private links in public/shared handouts.

Retention
How long should this entry stay visible?

Set review date, cleanup owner, archive rule, and deletion/escalation trigger before the record becomes stale.

Revisit
What would change the decision?

Name the model/tool change, policy change, incident, quality drift, or stakeholder concern that reopens the review.

Audit pattern

Make traceability lightweight enough to actually happen

1 · Start from the boundary

Use the approved scope

  • Which data category was allowed?
  • Which autonomy rung applied?
  • Which workflow owner was accountable?
2 · Record the human decision

The trail is not just a log dump

  • Who reviewed the AI-assisted output?
  • What rubric or acceptance rule was used?
  • What changed because of review?
3 · Link to approved evidence

Use pointers over payloads

  • Reference sanitized ticket IDs or doc names.
  • Keep raw logs in approved systems only.
  • Mark unknown evidence as a blocker.
4 · Add a revisit trigger

Prevent stale confidence

  • New model, vendor, policy, or access route.
  • Quality drift, incident, or support spike.
  • Review date reached with no owner.
