Parking-lot rule

Capture the follow-up shape, not the confidential story.

Do not write down customer names, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, contracts, pricing, private repository links, raw prompts, confidential strategy, credentials, regulated data, or attendee-specific notes. Keep only the public-safe question pattern, why it was parked, who should own the follow-up, and what evidence would make it safe to revisit.

Parking reasons

Choose why this question cannot be answered now

1 · Needs owner

No accountable role yet

Park questions that require a workflow owner, reviewer, sponsor, procurement contact, or policy owner before anyone can safely act.

2 · Needs sample

No safe example yet

Park questions that need toy data, fictional cases, redacted documents, or an approved sample before a tool can be tested.

3 · Needs boundary

Policy or data uncertainty

Park questions where the next answer depends on security, legal, compliance, procurement, or data-use boundaries.

4 · Needs evidence

Interesting, but unproven

Park questions that need a benchmark, quality check, review rubric, adoption signal, or decision criterion before a recommendation is useful.

5 · Too broad

Needs narrowing first

Park questions that are really several questions. Split them into one workflow pattern, one safe sample, and one next resource.

6 · Stop for now

Risk is higher than the room can resolve

Park questions that would require sensitive details or production impact. Route them to a policy exception, incident pause, or cleanup path.

Facilitator prompts

Keep the room moving

If time is short

  • Name the parking reason.
  • Assign the owner role, not a private person.
  • Point to the next public-safe resource.

If the question is sensitive

  • Stop detail collection.
  • Restate the question generically.
  • Route to boundary or policy review.

If follow-up is needed

  • Use the email template.
  • Include only hub links and safe sample instructions.
  • Ask for one reversible next action.
