Safe update rule

Share the decision evidence, not the private work.

Keep the update safe to forward by naming the workflow type, observed signal, sanitized artifact category, risk status, and next ask. Do not paste customer data, employee details, private repository links, credentials, production screenshots, vendor pricing, account pages, or internal strategy into a broadly shared update.

Update structure

Use six short sections

1. One-line status

Say whether the pilot is on track, needs clarification, paused, or ready for the next small step.

2. Workflow tested

Name the business workflow and user role in generic terms, such as “internal draft review” or “support-note summarization.”

3. Evidence observed

List the artifact, comparison, review signal, time saved, quality issue found, or adoption signal that changed the decision.

4. Risks and boundaries

Summarize the top risk row and what the pilot will not touch yet: real customers, production actions, credentials, regulated data, or irreversible changes.

5. Ask or decision needed

Ask for one concrete thing: approve the next safe sample, provide a reviewer, clarify policy, narrow scope, or stop the pilot.

6. Next review date

Name when the next evidence check happens and what signal will decide continue, clarify, or stop.

Status language

Choose a plain status

Continue

Useful signal, safe boundary

The pilot produced a useful artifact, the risk boundary is intact, and the next step remains reversible.

Clarify

Promise, but one gap

The pilot may be useful, but needs a reviewer, policy answer, better fixture, clearer owner, or sharper acceptance signal.

Pause

Unsafe or unsupported

The next meaningful test requires sensitive data, production access, external action, unclear ownership, or a policy decision first.

Copyable update

Paste this into email, Slack, or meeting notes

AI pilot status: [continue / clarify / pause] — [one sentence reason]

Workflow tested: [generic workflow and user role; no customer or account details]

Evidence observed: [sanitized artifact, quality signal, comparison baseline, or adoption signal]

Current boundary: [what the pilot is not touching yet]

Top risk: [risk category + mitigation or owner]

Ask: [one decision, reviewer, policy clarification, sample, or stop call]

Next review: [date] — continue if [signal], clarify if [signal], stop if [signal]

Facilitator cue

When to use it in the workshop

After scoring

Turn a score into communication

Use the update when attendees need to explain why a tool trial should continue, clarify, or stop without sending the full worksheet around.

After risk review

Show guardrails visibly

Pair the update with the risk register so stakeholders see the boundary, owner, and stop condition before approving any expansion.

Before the next meeting

Keep momentum small

Ask for one next reversible step instead of broad approval for production access, real customer data, or a vague transformation program.
