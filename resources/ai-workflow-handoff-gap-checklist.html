Handoff rule

If nobody can name the owner, reviewer, support route, and pause trigger, the workflow is not handed off.

Keep this page public-safe. Use fictional, toy, redacted, or approved examples only. Do not paste customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, credentials, regulated data, vendor contracts, pricing, raw prompts, private repository links, incident details, or confidential strategy.

Step 1

Check five continuity gaps

Ownership

Who is accountable?

Name the workflow owner, backup, reviewer, support route, and decision forum. “The team” is not an owner.

Review

What gets checked?

Confirm the output, allowed inputs, review frequency, acceptance criteria, and escalation rules are specific enough for a new reviewer.

Recovery

How does it pause?

Define the pause trigger, rollback action, communication owner, cleanup step, and restart condition before an incident forces improvisation.

Step 2

Classify every gap as close, monitor, or pause

Close before handoff

A missing owner, reviewer, support route, allowed-input rule, or pause trigger should be fixed before routine use starts.

Monitor during the next cycle

A weak metric, support theme, training need, or reviewer-friction signal can move forward if it has an owner and review date.

Pause and clarify

A data-boundary ambiguity, repeated unsafe output, missing approval, unmanaged incident path, or unclear rollback owner should stop expansion.

Archive as a lesson

If the workflow is no longer useful, record the reusable pattern, remove access, notify affected users, and keep only public-safe lessons.

Step 3

Use AI to draft a safe gap list

Gap-finding prompt: “Using only the public-safe workflow summary below, identify handoff gaps before this AI-assisted workflow becomes routine. Return gaps across ownership, reviewer process, support route, monitoring signal, pause/rollback path, and next review date. Classify each gap as close-before-handoff, monitor-next-cycle, or pause-and-clarify. Do not invent private examples, customer names, employee data, production logs, pricing, credentials, approvals, incident details, or private repository links.”

When to use this

Good moments for the handout

Before operations handoff

Run it after rollout readiness so the handoff packet does not hide missing support, monitoring, or rollback details.

After a health review

Convert quality, support, usage, or incident signals into explicit close, monitor, pause, or archive actions.

Before sponsor or steering updates

Show whether the team has operational continuity, not just an exciting demo or one successful sample.
