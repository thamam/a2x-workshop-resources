Turn AI evidence into one clear decision meeting.

Use this agenda after a demo, reference check, small trial, or pilot review. It keeps the conversation anchored on workflow evidence, data boundaries, owners, and the next reversible step instead of hype, anxiety, or private examples.

Meeting rule

If the evidence is not ready, decide what to learn next.

Do not turn a weak demo, vague reference, or single impressive run into a broad rollout decision. If the team cannot name the workflow, allowed inputs, observed evidence, owner, risk boundary, and rollback path, the decision is clarify first, not expand.

Before the meeting

Bring a sanitized decision packet

Decision ask

Choose exactly one ask: run a tiny trial, continue the pilot, clarify one blocker, expand narrowly, pause, stop, or archive.

Evidence summary

Bring counts, pass/fail signals, reviewer decisions, friction notes, and representative patterns. Do not bring raw customer data, account screenshots, contracts, production logs, or private repository links.

Boundary and owner

Include allowed inputs, off-limits data, the accountable owner, the human reviewer, and who can pause the workflow if the boundary fails.

30-minute agenda

Keep the room moving

Restate the workflow and decision ask

What repeated job is under discussion, who benefits, and what decision must be made today?

Review evidence, not anecdotes

Summarize the proof: observed runs, quality signal, time saved or friction removed, reviewer confidence, setup effort, and unresolved gaps.

Confirm the boundary

Check allowed inputs, redaction, retention, access, escalation, and off-limits categories before any team assumes real data is approved.

Name ownership and support

Confirm accountable owner, backup, reviewer, support route, review cadence, and pause condition before the workflow gets wider attention.

Choose one next state

Pick continue, clarify, expand narrowly, pause, stop, or archive. Write the reason in one sentence and name the evidence that would change the decision later.

Decision states

Use plain language

Continue

The pilot stays narrow.

Evidence is useful enough to keep learning, but the allowed workflow, inputs, users, and review gate remain constrained.

Clarify

One blocker must be resolved.

The next step is not rollout; it is answering a named question about safety, quality, ownership, support, integration, or user adoption.

Expand narrowly

The next audience is controlled.

Only expand if the rollout checklist, owner card, rollback path, stakeholder message, and review date are ready.

Pause or stop

The evidence says not now.

Capture the reason, clean up access if needed, preserve sanitized lessons, and avoid letting a zombie pilot linger.
