Safe handoff rule

Operational handoff means supportable scope, not permanent permission.

Keep the handoff narrow: one workflow, named owner, allowed inputs, support route, monitoring signal, incident trigger, rollback owner, and review cadence. Do not paste customer data, employee records, production credentials, billing pages, private repository links, internal strategy, or vendor contracts into a broadly shared handoff document.

Handoff gates

Eight gates before the pilot becomes routine work

1. Owner gate

Who owns the workflow after the pilot team steps back, and who can pause it if the evidence changes?

2. User gate

Which users are trained, which users are excluded, and what is the route for access questions?

3. Input gate

Which input classes are allowed, sanitized, fictional, or prohibited in routine use?

4. Monitoring gate

What signal says the workflow is healthy: review sample, failure count, escalation rate, time saved, or quality note?

5. Support gate

Where do users report confusion, bad output, policy questions, or access issues without exposing sensitive material?

6. Incident gate

What triggers a pause: sensitive input, unsafe output, repeated hallucination, tool failure, policy concern, or user confusion?

7. Rollback gate

Who can disable the workflow, remove access, revert a rule, switch to manual process, or publish the pause message?

8. Improvement gate

When will owners review evidence and decide whether to tune prompts, update rules, expand scope, narrow scope, or stop?

Handoff states

Choose one operational state

Ready for operations

Owner, support, and rollback are named

Move into routine use with bounded scope, trained users, monitoring signal, and a scheduled review.

Handoff incomplete

One gate needs a concrete owner

Do not expand the operating routine until support, incident, monitoring, or rollback ownership is explicit.

Return to pilot mode

Routine use would hide too much risk

Keep the workflow in pilot mode if ownership, user training, input policy, or pause authority is unclear.
