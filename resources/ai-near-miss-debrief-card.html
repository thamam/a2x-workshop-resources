Near-miss rule

Capture the pattern, decision, and prevention move — not the sensitive raw event.

A near-miss debrief is for learning before harm repeats. Keep customer names, employee details, account screenshots, production logs, raw prompts, transcripts, credentials, vendor-confidential data, and regulated records out of the worksheet. Store any real incident evidence only in an approved internal system.

Debrief lanes

Separate signal from gossip

Trigger
What made someone pause?

Name the generic signal: wrong data category, unsupported claim, skipped reviewer, surprising action, policy uncertainty, or user confusion.

Boundary
Which guardrail almost failed?

Identify the boundary type: input, output, permission, review, routing, retention, vendor, or communication.

Catch point
Where was it caught?

Record the human review step, system check, teammate question, test fixture, or customer-safe rehearsal that prevented a worse outcome.

Learning
What pattern should the team remember?

Turn the event into a reusable rule, checklist item, prompt constraint, review cue, or permissions-ladder adjustment.

Prevention
What changes before the next run?

Pick one small change: narrower input, stronger rubric, forced reviewer, blocked action, tool setting, owner decision, or stop trigger.

Escalation
Does this need formal handling?

If the near miss may involve protected data, regulated work, legal duties, customer impact, security exposure, or HR decisions, stop the workshop worksheet and use the approved internal process.

Debrief pattern

Run a fifteen-minute learning loop

1 · Stabilize

Keep the workflow paused or contained

  • Do not widen access or permissions.
  • Confirm whether any real incident process is needed.
  • Move sensitive evidence to approved systems only.
2 · Abstract

Describe the safe pattern

  • Use role and data categories, not names.
  • Describe the workflow step, not private records.
  • Capture what caught the issue.
3 · Change one control

Make prevention concrete

  • Add a prompt constraint or test case.
  • Tighten review criteria or permissions.
  • Name the owner and next check date.
4 · Decide restart conditions

Do not resume on vibes

  • Define what must be true before the next run.
  • Record who approves the restart.
  • Keep escalation open if facts are uncertain.

Good next moves

Connect learning to the rest of the field kit

If the issue is active

Use the incident pause card first. Do not use this worksheet as a substitute for a security, legal, HR, customer, or compliance process.

If the workflow can be tested safely

Return to the shadow-mode trial card, output review rubric, data boundary checklist, and acceptance criteria before expanding use.

If the team needs proof

Record only safe evidence pointers in the audit trail starter or pilot evidence log, then revisit permissions and rollback drills before restarting.
