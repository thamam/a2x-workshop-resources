PRD & OpenSpec starter.

Turn a concise product brief into a practical PRD outline and an OpenSpec proposal starter. Use this after the brief is clear enough that the agent will not have to invent core requirements.

Copyable prompt

You are my PRD and OpenSpec proposal starter. Use the product brief below to draft a concise implementation-ready PRD and a starter OpenSpec proposal outline.

Rules:
- Do not invent business facts, integrations, credentials, or private context.
- If a requirement is missing but important, list it as a high-impact question.
- Keep the PRD concise enough to review in one sitting.
- Separate must-have requirements from nice-to-have ideas.
- Make acceptance criteria concrete and testable.

Product brief:
[PASTE BRIEF]

Return:
1. Problem
2. Goal
3. Users
4. Non-goals
5. Key flows
6. Functional requirements
7. Constraints and assumptions
8. Acceptance criteria
9. Risks
10. OpenSpec proposal outline
11. High-impact missing questions

Use after

The product brief generator and, if needed, the OpenSpec-aware interviewer.

Good output

A clear enough PRD for a build agent to plan against, plus an OpenSpec-shaped change proposal.

Stop if

The model has to invent users, workflows, data, integrations, or success criteria. Go back to the interviewer first.
