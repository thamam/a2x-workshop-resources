Close an AI trial without leaving sensitive leftovers or vague decisions behind.

Cleanup rule

Keep approved evidence, not messy copies of real work.

Do not paste or preserve secrets, customer records, employee data, production logs, raw prompts, raw outputs, transcripts, account screenshots, credentials, regulated records, contracts, private repository links, pricing, or confidential strategy in this worksheet. Use generic categories here and store any real cleanup evidence only in approved internal systems.

Cleanup passes

Walk the trial through six simple decisions

1 · Inventory

Name the trial artifacts

List artifact categories only: prompts, sample inputs, generated outputs, review notes, screenshots, exports, vendor workspace items, temporary accounts, or shared documents.

2 · Keep

Retain approved evidence

Keep only sanitized decision evidence: workflow goal, allowed input categories, review outcome, pass/fail reason, owner, and next-review date.

3 · Remove

Route sensitive leftovers

Flag raw prompts, logs, transcripts, customer examples, account screenshots, exports, or temporary copies for deletion through the normal approved process.

4 · Access

Close accounts and links

Check temporary users, shared links, test repositories, vendor demo spaces, tokens, environment variables, and local files without recording secret values here.

5 · Decision

Stop, narrow, or continue

Decide whether the trial stops, reruns with safer inputs, continues in shadow mode, moves to policy review, or needs sponsor/procurement/security approval.

6 · Owner

Assign cleanup follow-through

Name the role responsible for cleanup evidence, follow-up questions, next trial boundaries, and the date when unresolved items expire or escalate.

What good looks like

Evidence without exposure

Safe to keep here

  • Generic artifact categories.
  • Sanitized pass/fail rationale.
  • Owner roles and review dates.
  • Links to public-safe hub resources.

Store elsewhere only if approved

  • Real deletion tickets or audit logs.
  • Vendor export/deletion confirmations.
  • Internal access reviews.
  • Policy, legal, security, or procurement decisions.

Never put in this worksheet

  • Secrets, credentials, or tokens.
  • Customer, employee, or regulated records.
  • Raw prompts, outputs, transcripts, or logs.
  • Private repo URLs, contracts, pricing, or confidential strategy.
