Safe ownership rule

Name responsibility without exposing sensitive work.

Keep the card generic: roles, review cadence, escalation paths, and stop conditions are enough. Do not paste customer records, employee data, production credentials, billing pages, account screenshots, private repository links, raw tickets, internal strategy, vendor contracts, or unredacted incident transcripts into a shared owner card.

Owner-card fields

Seven fields that make the workflow supportable

1. Accountable owner

Who can say yes, no, pause, or narrow the workflow when evidence changes?

2. Backup owner

Who covers review, escalation, or rollback when the owner is unavailable?

3. Human reviewer

Who checks outputs before they affect customers, money, commitments, access, or production systems?

4. Support route

Where do users report confusion, unsafe output, access problems, or workflow friction without sharing secrets?

5. Review cadence

When does the owner inspect evidence: after five runs, weekly, monthly, or before every expansion?

6. Escalation boundary

Which events require escalation: sensitive input, repeated low-quality output, policy concern, incident, or unclear accountability?

7. Pause condition

What exact evidence triggers pause, rollback, manual fallback, or a return to pilot mode?

Ownership states

Choose the honest state before moving forward

Owned

Owner, reviewer, support, and pause rule are named

The workflow may proceed to the next gated step with evidence review and bounded scope.

Almost owned

One field still needs a real role

Continue only as a narrow pilot until the missing owner, backup, reviewer, support route, or pause condition is named.

Unowned

No one can pause or repair it

Do not scale, automate, or make it routine. Return to use-case intake, risk register, or manual fallback.

Facilitator cue

When to use it in the workshop

After intake

Turn interest into accountability

Use this right after the use-case intake brief if the next question is “who will own this if it works?”

Before pilot planning

Prevent orphan pilots

Require an owner card before a team scores tools, collects pilot evidence, or asks for broader rollout.

During operations

Repair fuzzy handoffs

If support or pause authority is unclear, refresh the owner card before changing prompts, scope, or access.
