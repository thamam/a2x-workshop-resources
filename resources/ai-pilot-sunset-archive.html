A good AI pilot can still deserve a clean stop.

Use this public-safe checklist when health review evidence says an AI workflow should be retired, paused, replaced, or archived. The goal is to close the loop without losing lessons, confusing users, or leaving risky access and stale instructions behind.

Safe closure rule

Archive decisions and patterns, not sensitive artifacts.

Keep the closure packet shareable: record decision evidence, owners, generic workflow names, risk categories, access changes, and lessons. Do not paste customer data, employee records, account screenshots, production credentials, private repository links, vendor contracts, raw logs, or unredacted incident transcripts into a broad archive.

Closure triggers

Use this when one of these signals appears

Stop

Risk outpaces value

Incidents, unsafe input pressure, support burden, or unclear accountability now outweigh the workflow's observed benefit.

Pause

Evidence is incomplete

The workflow may still be useful, but ownership, review gates, allowed inputs, or adoption evidence need repair before routine use continues.

Replace

A better pattern exists

The pilot taught the team enough to move into a simpler workflow, narrower prompt, different tool, or human-owned process.

Checklist

Eight steps for clean retirement

1. Name the closure decision

Choose one state: retire, pause, replace, narrow, or archive as complete. Tie it to health review or retrospective evidence.

2. Notify current users

Say what changes, when it changes, who owns questions, and what safe alternative to use next.

3. Remove or freeze access

Disable optional automations, shortcuts, shared prompts, credentials, integrations, or bookmarked instructions that could keep stale use alive.

4. Capture sanitized evidence

Archive counts, categories, decisions, and examples that are fictionalized or redacted enough for broad internal learning.

5. Preserve reusable lessons

Keep the rule, prompt pattern, review gate, training note, or support lesson that should influence the next pilot.

6. Assign archive ownership

Name who owns the closure packet, where the safe summary lives, and when it can be deleted or revisited.

7. Update downstream docs

Remove the retired workflow from onboarding, status reports, routing docs, demos, and recurring review calendars.

8. Decide the next small move

Choose one reversible next action: replacement pilot, narrowed retry, policy update, training note, or no further action.

Facilitator cue

When to use it in the workshop

After health review

Give stopping a workflow

Use this after the health review page so teams learn that responsible AI adoption includes retirement, not only rollout.

Before a replacement pilot

Avoid stale parallel workflows

Have the team close or freeze the old path before presenting the next tool, prompt, or automation as the new default.

When sponsors ask “what happened?”

Turn closure into credibility

Use the closure packet to show evidence, safety, and learning without turning a stopped pilot into a failure narrative.
