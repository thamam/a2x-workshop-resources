Citation rule

No quote, no confidence.

If an AI answer cannot point to a short source excerpt, named assumption, or visible uncertainty note, treat it as a draft hypothesis. Do not turn it into a decision, external message, customer claim, procurement answer, legal/compliance statement, or production action.

Three support lanes

Sort each claim before trusting it.

Supported

The answer quotes or points to a safe source.

The claim is tied to a copied excerpt, public doc, sanitized note, accepted decision record, or reviewed artifact. The reviewer can inspect the source without needing private credentials or confidential material.

Assumed

The answer fills a gap.

The model inferred missing context, generalized from examples, or used a pattern that may be useful but is not proven. Mark the assumption and ask what evidence would confirm it.

Unsupported

The answer sounds precise but has no source.

Numbers, policies, customer facts, commitments, contract details, risk ratings, names, or deadlines without source support should be removed, revised, or escalated before use.

Five-minute check

Run this before sharing the answer.

Split the answer into claims.

Highlight each factual statement, recommendation, decision, number, owner, deadline, risk, or promise. Do not check the whole answer as one blob.

Attach the shortest safe source.

Use a public doc, toy example, redacted excerpt, reviewed note, or accepted decision record. Do not paste customer data, employee records, secrets, private repository links, contracts, account screenshots, regulated records, or confidential strategy into the check.

Mark assumptions honestly.

If the source does not say it, label the claim as an assumption or question. Ask the model to separate supported facts from guesses instead of polishing the gap away.

Choose the allowed next step.

Supported claims may move to review. Assumptions need confirmation. Unsupported or sensitive claims must be removed, rewritten as questions, or escalated to the right owner.

Save only public-safe evidence.

Keep generic claim categories, safe excerpts, reviewer decisions, and open questions. Leave raw transcripts, private tickets, customer records, employee data, vendor contracts, pricing, and legal/compliance conclusions out of shared workshop artifacts.

Where this fits

Use it at the moments where plausibility is dangerous.

After meeting summaries

Check that decisions, owners, and action items came from the notes instead of model inference.

Before stakeholder updates

Separate observed evidence from optimistic phrasing before sharing progress, risk, or adoption signals.

During vendor or policy answers

Keep procurement, security, legal, pricing, and data-use claims as questions unless source material and owner approval exist.
