Shadow-mode rule

AI can suggest, score, or draft — but the existing workflow remains the source of truth.

A shadow-mode trial is not a launch. Run the AI workflow on fictional, toy, redacted, or approved samples first; then compare its suggested output with the human or existing-process result. Do not let the AI tool send messages, update records, commit code, approve spend, change customer state, route employee decisions, or replace a required reviewer until the exit criteria, owner, and approval path are explicit.

Trial lanes

Define the test before anyone calls it working

Workflow slice
What tiny repeated task is being shadowed?

Pick one narrow step, one owner role, and one expected output. Avoid testing the whole department at once.

Safe sample
What data may the AI see?

Use toy examples, fictional cases, public docs, or approved redacted samples. Treat missing safe samples as a blocker.

Comparison
What is the baseline result?

Compare AI output to the current human or system result using the same input category and a named review rubric.

Decision lane
What can the AI output influence today?

Usually: nothing beyond reviewer learning, notes, and evidence collection. Humans keep final action authority.

Stop trigger
What ends the trial immediately?

Unsafe input, hallucinated evidence, policy uncertainty, reviewer overload, stakeholder concern, or quality below threshold.

Exit rule
What proof is needed before the next permission rung?

Name the pass rate, review sample size, owner sign-off, failure examples, rollback path, and approval forum.

Trial pattern

Run the smallest useful comparison loop

1 · Freeze the baseline

Know what “better” means

  • Current cycle time or effort.
  • Current quality/rework signal.
  • Current reviewer or owner role.
2 · Shadow with safe input

Keep AI behind glass

  • Use only approved sample categories.
  • Label every AI output as not final.
  • Do not send, merge, approve, or update records.
3 · Compare with a rubric

Review misses, not vibes

  • Task fit and evidence support.
  • Boundary and tone safety.
  • Operational clarity and reversibility.
4 · Decide the next rung

Promote slowly or stop cleanly

  • Continue shadow mode.
  • Narrow the workflow or sample.
  • Escalate, pause, or request approval.

During the trial
