Safe review rule

Review health signals, not sensitive work samples.

Keep the review shareable: describe counts, categories, decisions, and sanitized examples. Do not paste customer data, employee records, production credentials, account screenshots, private repository links, vendor contracts, or raw incident transcripts into a broadly shared health review.

Review checks

Seven signals for a recurring health review

1. Usage signal

Is the workflow being used by the intended people, at the intended cadence, for the intended narrow use case?

2. Quality signal

Are outputs still good enough after human review, or are users correcting the same failure pattern repeatedly?

3. Safety signal

Have allowed-input rules, review gates, escalation rules, or prohibited-use boundaries drifted since handoff?

4. Support signal

Are questions, access issues, confusing outputs, and tool failures reaching the right owner quickly enough?

5. Incident signal

Were there pauses, sensitive-input near misses, bad outputs, policy questions, tool outages, or rollback events?

6. Value signal

Is the workflow still saving time, improving consistency, reducing handoff friction, or producing useful evidence?

7. Improvement signal

What should change before the next review: prompt, rule, input scope, owner, training note, monitoring, or stop condition?

Health states

Choose one state before widening scope

Healthy

Keep running within current scope

Usage, quality, support, and incidents are within the expected boundary. Capture one small improvement and review again.

Needs tuning

Improve before expansion

The workflow is useful, but a repeated issue needs a prompt, rule, training, support, or monitoring update before wider use.

Pause or narrow

Risk or confusion exceeds value

Pause expansion or narrow the use case when incidents, support load, unsafe inputs, or unclear ownership outpace the benefit.

Facilitator cue

When to use it in the workshop

After operations handoff

Make routine use observable

Use this after ownership is named so the team has a simple way to notice value, drift, and support issues.

Before expanding scope

Require evidence before widening

Have the owner bring one review note before adding more users, inputs, automations, or integrations.

When confidence is vague

Separate health from optimism

If the team says “it seems fine,” turn that into usage, quality, safety, support, incident, and value signals.
