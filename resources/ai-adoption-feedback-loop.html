A2X field kit · adoption improvement

Turn adoption feedback into safe improvement decisions.

Use this handout after enablement, office hours, health reviews, support check-ins, or steering updates. It helps teams collect sanitized feedback patterns, separate fixable friction from policy boundaries, and choose one reviewed improvement without exposing private examples.

Feedback-loop rule

Collect patterns, not private stories; improve one reversible thing at a time.

Do not collect raw chats, customer records, employee details, production logs, account screenshots, private repository contents, credentials, regulated records, vendor contracts, pricing claims, or internal strategy. Capture friction categories, support themes, review signals, guardrail questions, and improvement candidates. If a fix needs sensitive evidence or policy approval, route it to the approved owner instead of pasting details into the worksheet.

Step 1

Gather safe feedback signals

Use

Sanitized patterns

Record themes such as confusing step, missing reviewer, slow handoff, unclear allowed input, weak output, support repeat, or training gap.

Summarize

Operational friction

Group repeated issues by workflow stage, role, guardrail, support path, or evidence gap instead of preserving private examples.

Exclude

Sensitive artifacts

Leave out raw prompts, transcripts, tickets, contracts, screenshots, customer names, employee records, private URLs, credentials, and regulated data.

Step 2

Classify the feedback before acting

Training or explanation issue

Users understand the boundary but need a clearer example, shorter instruction, reminder, FAQ answer, or office-hours walkthrough.

Workflow design issue

The handoff, reviewer role, allowed input, output format, or evidence gate is causing friction and can be changed without expanding scope.

Tool or model behavior issue

The output is inconsistent, a vendor/model changed, or a workflow needs rerun with a safe sample before continuing.

Boundary or approval issue

The request needs different data, policy, procurement, legal, security, or sponsor approval. Do not solve it with copy edits.

Step 3

Choose one reviewed improvement

Decision prompt: “Using only the sanitized feedback patterns below, propose one small improvement to the AI workflow. Return: feedback theme, likely cause, proposed change, owner role, review evidence, rollback or pause condition, and what remains unresolved. Do not invent private examples, metrics, commitments, pricing, approvals, or user claims.”

Copyable worksheet

Feedback loop note

Workflow: [generic workflow name and current adoption state].

Feedback source: [office hours / support check-in / health review / steering / enablement].

Sanitized themes: [friction category, count or frequency if safe, affected role].

Classification: [training / workflow design / tool behavior / boundary approval].

One proposed improvement: [small reversible change, owner role, review evidence].

Stop or escalation trigger: [sensitive data needed / repeated failure / unclear owner / policy boundary].

Do not include: raw chats, customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, private repository contents, credentials, regulated records, vendor contracts, pricing claims, raw prompts, or internal strategy.

When to use this

After a health review
