Commitment rule

Commit to the next evidence check, not to rollout.

Do not collect names, customer records, employee data, production logs, account screenshots, contracts, vendor pricing, private repository links, raw prompts, confidential strategy, credentials, or regulated records. Capture the workflow pattern, safe sample, owner role, reviewer role, evidence signal, decision date, and stop condition for one small reversible move.

Commitment lanes

Pick the smallest honest next step

1 · Clarify

Still too vague

Use discovery or intake before anyone tests a tool. The commitment is to answer one missing workflow, boundary, owner, or reviewer question.

2 · Try

Safe sample exists

Use a toy repo, fictional document, or sanitized sample. The commitment is to run one reversible exercise and inspect the output together.

3 · Review

Output quality is uncertain

Use a rubric, calibration session, or acceptance criteria. The commitment is to define pass, revise, and stop thresholds before reuse.

4 · Govern

Policy or data boundary is unclear

Use data-boundary, prompt-safety, or policy-exception resources. The commitment is to name the approval path without exposing sensitive details.

5 · Decide

A sponsor decision is needed

Use sponsor alignment, steering cadence, or decision log. The commitment is to prepare one safe decision packet, not to promise adoption.

6 · Pause

The safe next step is no

Use incident, rollback, cleanup, or sunset aids. The commitment is to stop, narrow, or park the workflow until the missing boundary exists.

Facilitator cues

Keep the commitment small enough to be real

If people are excited

  • Ask for one workflow, not a transformation program.
  • Make the safe sample explicit.
  • Choose the reviewer before the tool.

If people are nervous

  • Respect the risk as a boundary signal.
  • Commit to evidence or approval first.
  • Make pause a valid outcome.

If people want buying advice

  • Separate workflow evidence from vendor preference.
  • Avoid pricing or guarantee claims.
  • Route to demo brief, scorecard, or procurement prep.
