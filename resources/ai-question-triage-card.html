Triage rule

Answer the route, not the fantasy.

Do not collect customer data, employee records, production logs, account screenshots, contracts, pricing, private repository links, raw prompts, confidential strategy, credentials, or regulated records while triaging a question. Capture the workflow pattern, risk boundary, safe sample idea, owner role, and next public-safe resource only.

Question lanes

Sort the question before recommending a tool

1 · Teach

“How does this agent stuff work?”

Route to the harness map, CLAUDE.md rules, or first-skill pages. Keep the answer conceptual until the attendee has a safe toy target.

2 · Discover

“Where could we use AI?”

Route to discovery questions, prioritization, or use-case intake. Ask for repeated work, safe sample categories, review gates, and owner roles.

3 · Try

“Can we test this next week?”

Route to the action plan, experiment charter, data boundary checklist, or prompt-safety preflight. Keep the first trial tiny and reversible.

4 · Buy or demo

“Which tool/vendor should we pick?”

Route to the tool scorecard, vendor demo brief, reference check, data-use questions, or procurement prep. Avoid claims and pricing details.

5 · Govern

“Who approves or owns this?”

Route to owner card, permissions ladder, policy exception request, sponsor alignment, steering cadence, or decision log before expanding scope.

6 · Stop

“This might be risky.”

Route to prompt-safety, incident pause, near-miss debrief, rollback drill, or cleanup checklist. Stop collection of sensitive details immediately.

Fast routing examples

Use the smallest useful answer

If the question is vague

  • Ask for the repeated workflow.
  • Name the safe sample category.
  • Open discovery or use-case intake.

If the question is excited

  • Keep the promise narrow.
  • Pick one reversible experiment.
  • Route to action plan or experiment charter.

If the question is risky

  • Stop sensitive detail collection.
  • Name the boundary.
  • Route to policy exception, pause, or cleanup.
