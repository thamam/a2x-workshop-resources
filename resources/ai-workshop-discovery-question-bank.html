Discovery rule

A good AI use case starts as a repeated workflow, not a tool wish.

Keep this page public-safe. Ask for roles, workflow patterns, toy examples, fictional samples, or redacted categories only. Do not collect customer data, employee records, production logs, credentials, account screenshots, regulated data, vendor contracts, pricing, private repository links, raw prompts, incident transcripts, or confidential strategy.

Question bank

Five safe discovery lenses

Workflow pull

Where does work repeat?

  • What do people rewrite, summarize, check, route, or hand off every week?
  • Which step is annoying enough that someone already made a workaround?
  • What would be useful even if AI only drafted the first pass?
Safe sample

Can we practice without sensitive data?

  • What toy, fictional, synthetic, or redacted example looks like the work?
  • What must stay out of the prompt, repository, transcript, or demo?
  • Who decides when real data is allowed later?
Review gate

Who can judge the output?

  • What does a good answer, draft, summary, or code change need to include?
  • Who can approve, revise, escalate, or pause the output?
  • What evidence would make the reviewer comfortable with the next step?
Reversibility

How do we keep the first move small?

  • What is the smallest workflow slice we can test in one session?
  • What action is reversible if the output is wrong?
  • What stop condition prevents a demo from becoming accidental rollout?
Ownership

Who owns the next decision?

  • Who is the workflow owner, reviewer, support route, and escalation path?
  • What decision is needed before the next experiment?
  • What question must remain unanswered until an approved boundary exists?
Facilitator sequence

Run a ten-minute discovery pass

Start with the repeated moment

Ask for a generic workflow pattern: “What work keeps coming back?” Avoid names, live records, private repositories, customer examples, or screenshots.

Translate it into a safe sample

Ask what toy or fictional example would be close enough to test the behavior. If the answer requires sensitive data, stop and make boundary approval the next task.

Name the human judgment

Ask who can tell whether the AI output is usable, what evidence they need, and what “revise” or “pause” would look like.

Choose the next handout

If there are many candidates, open the prioritization matrix. If one candidate is clear, open the use-case intake brief or post-workshop action plan.
