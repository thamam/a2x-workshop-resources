Office-hours rule

Support the pattern, not the private artifact.

Do not troubleshoot with customer records, employee data, contracts, regulated data, production logs, account screenshots, private repository contents, credentials, vendor pricing, incident transcripts, or internal strategy. Ask people to bring a generic workflow description, sanitized symptom, toy input, or redacted screenshot that proves the question without exposing the underlying material.

Step 1

Decide what office hours are allowed to handle

Good fit

Workflow questions

Clarify when to use the AI workflow, how to prepare safe inputs, where human review happens, and what evidence to capture.

Good fit

Support friction

Collect repeated confusion about prompts, handoffs, ownership, review gates, pause triggers, or feedback routes.

Escalate

Boundary uncertainty

If a question needs sensitive data, new tool access, policy exception, procurement review, or a wider user group, route it to the named owner instead of solving it live.

Stop

Incident signals

If output drift, unsafe input, missing reviewer, support overload, or unclear restart authority appears, pause new use and switch to the incident pause card.

Step 2

Run a 30-minute adoption office-hours agenda

0–3 min · Restate the boundary

Remind attendees which workflow, user group, allowed inputs, review gate, and pause trigger are in scope today.

3–10 min · Triage questions safely

Sort questions into workflow clarity, prompt/use friction, review/ownership friction, boundary escalation, or incident signal. Keep examples generic.

10–20 min · Work one sanitized example

Use a toy input or redacted pattern to show the right prompt, review step, evidence note, and handoff without touching real sensitive material.

20–25 min · Capture improvement signals

Name repeated confusion, missing documentation, risky assumptions, support load, or guardrail drift that the workflow owner should review.

25–30 min · Confirm next actions

Assign owner actions, escalations, documentation updates, next office-hours date, and any pause/narrowing decision.

Step 3

Close each session with one support decision

Continue support rhythm

Questions are routine, boundaries are stable, reviewers are present, and users know the next review date.

Update the workflow aid

Users are confused in a repeated way. Update the prompt, checklist, route chooser, owner card, or enablement notes before the next session.

Narrow or pause use

Questions show unclear data boundaries, missing ownership, weak review gates, unsupported users, or incident signals. Narrow the workflow or pause until the owner reviews evidence.
