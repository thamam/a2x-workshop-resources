A2X field kit · incident pause

Pause the AI workflow before the risk becomes the process.

Use this card when an AI workflow produces unexpected output, touches an unclear boundary, creates review discomfort, or starts spreading faster than the guardrails. It helps the team stop cleanly, preserve sanitized evidence, assign owners, and choose a safe recovery path.

Pause rule

A good pause is reversible, owned, and evidence-based.

Do not paste raw prompts, customer records, employee data, contracts, regulated records, production logs, account screenshots, private repository contents, credentials, vendor pricing, incident transcripts, or internal strategy into the pause note. Record the workflow pattern, what changed, who paused it, what evidence is safe to share, and what decision is needed before restart.

Step 1

Check the pause triggers

Output drift

Results changed shape

The AI output is inconsistent, unreviewable, overconfident, or no longer matches the workflow definition.

Boundary doubt

Input or access feels unclear

The workflow may involve sensitive data, private repositories, production systems, customer material, employee data, or unapproved tools.

Ownership gap

No one can approve restart

The owner, reviewer, support route, rollback owner, or decision forum is missing or unavailable.

Adoption pressure

The pilot is spreading too fast

More users, inputs, or downstream decisions are relying on the workflow before evidence and guardrails are stable.

Step 2

Fill the six pause fields

1. Workflow paused

Name the workflow pattern, current stage, accountable owner, and reviewer using roles or generic team labels.

2. Trigger observed

Choose output drift, boundary doubt, ownership gap, adoption pressure, support issue, or another plain-language trigger.

3. Safe evidence

Record sanitized counts, pass/fail notes, toy examples, redacted excerpts, timestamps, or generic screenshots. If evidence is sensitive, name where it is stored without copying it into this note.

4. Immediate containment

Describe what stopped: new prompts, user access, automation, vendor sharing, publication, production action, or downstream decision use.

5. Recovery owner and reviewer

Name who investigates, who approves restart, who updates stakeholders, and who owns cleanup or rollback.

6. Restart condition

Define the evidence, guardrail, test, policy answer, or support change required before the workflow resumes.

Step 3

Choose the recovery state

Resume narrowly

The trigger is understood, evidence is safe, owner and reviewer are named, and the restart condition is met. Resume with a smaller scope and a review date.

Investigate first

The workflow still looks useful, but the evidence, boundary, owner, or guardrail is incomplete. Keep it paused while the owner runs a safe check.

Stop or escalate

The workflow needs sensitive inputs in an unapproved tool, has no rollback owner, affects production or public claims, or creates unclear accountability. Stop it or route to the policy exception path.
